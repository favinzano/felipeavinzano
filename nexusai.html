    Personal Statement — Asylum Narrative
    
            
    
              
    I declare the following statement to be true and correct to the best of my knowledge and belief. I am seeking asylum in the United States due to a well-founded fear of persecution in my home country.
    
              
    My difficulties began in early 2022 when I was targeted for my political activism and community leadership in my hometown. I was repeatedly threatened by local authorities who warned me to cease my advocacy.
    
              
    My difficulties began when I was targeted for my community organization work. The paramilitary group known as "La Vanguardia" began to see us as a political threat because our neighborhood meetings were not aligned with the ruling party.
    
              
    On January 12, 2024, two men in black intercepted me and pushed me into an alley. They beat me repeatedly on the ribs. One of them shouted: "This is for continuing as an opposition leader. Next time you won't get up." They left a pamphlet of "La Vanguardia" on my chest.
    
              
    On January 14, 2024, I went to the Central District police station to file a complaint (Report No. VER-992). The officer on duty laughed and told me: "We don't get involved with those people. If you look for trouble with politics, don't expect us to protect you."
    
              
    On June 5, 2024, a group of armed men arrived at my workplace asking for my current address. Thanks to a colleague who warned me by message, I was able to leave through the back door and hid at a relative's house for two weeks.
    
              
    I received a text message to my personal phone: "No matter where you hide, Veridia is small for us." It was then that I decided to flee to the United States to save my life.
